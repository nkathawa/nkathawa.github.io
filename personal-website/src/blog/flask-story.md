*"Wine hath drowned more men than the sea."*  
-Thomas Fuller

\*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

Cold steel. Moving parts. Shaping. Forming. Creating. Flask after flask moving along the conveyor belt. One falls into the box awaiting it below. A Jack Daniels boxed set, containing a fifth of Jack and a metallic grey flask. Onto a truck, delivered to Minneapolis, Minnesota. Then, to the University of Minnesota campus. Then, to the supermarket. Unpacked. Placed on the shelf. Waiting to be purchased, for $29.99 plus tax.

\*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

I awake to the same thing as the day before, and the day before that, and the day before that. The view of the freezer aisle, right next to the refrigerated foods aisle, right beside the bread. People pass by in droves, some tall, some short; some men, some women; some in shape, with nicely shaped bodies, some out of shape, fat and unsightly. There were little people too, I think they are called children. They seem to be full of innocence and joy. They are my favorite, with their antics and the way they seem to love life. They are so full of hope, to me. They will grow to be the size of the normal people, I assume, meaning they have so much time ahead of them to grow, mature, and learn.

There are also people who look to be the same size as the adult people, but somehow seem less mature. They seem to be less acclimated to the world. They come less often; they purchase fewer things. They often buy mac and cheese, ramen, peanut butter, frozen chicken, alcohol. These are, I think, the main staples of this type of person’s diet. I have essentially categorized this type of people by their purchases; I call them half-dones. That’s because they aren’t as small as the children, but they are not yet at the level of the adults. They still have a lot of learning to do, I assume, based on the way they behave.

I’ve been on this shelf now for two months, and it’s been lonely. I’m waiting patiently, for the day I will be purchased, and finally be used, for whatever I was made for. I can tell that I am hollow on the inside, and I see I am in this box with some kind of drink, so I assume I am here to be filled with drink and drunk from. Which sounds great to me; anything is better than sitting on this shelf.

Just now, a group of half-dones walks by. They are joking and laughing with each other, as I have seen groups of them do in the past. It is late in the day, I think…I can’t really see the outside from here, so the way I tell time is by the amount of people that pass by per minute. Right now, it’s at about five people per minute, which means it’s likely about ten o’clock at night. Closing soon, at eleven. The half-dones walk by, and head to the plastic-ware aisle. They then come back with some red cups, and small plastic glasses. Then, they approach me, and look at the items around me.

One picks up a fifth of Jack Daniels; the others grab bottles of wine and six packs of beer. As they are about to leave, one of the half-dones, tall, brown haired, and physically fit, says to the boy holding the Jack, “hey Dave, that’s the same price as this boxed set, so I’m gonna snag this instead.”

“Sounds good, Bill,” Dave replies. Dave replaces the fifth, and Bill reaches toward me, and grabs the box I am in. I’m finally being purchased. 

\*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

The feeling of hands touching me is quite new; I’m not sure I like it yet. Bill is holding me now, analyzing me, studying my material and makeup. There are a lot of people at his home right now. Music is playing, and the people are jumping up and down, or standing in circles, or pressed up against each other. Bill takes me up the stairs to his room, and places me on his bedside table. He then leaves the room.

I sit, alone, waiting to be filled up and drunk from. The music goes on downstairs; eventually it dies down. Bill reenters the room—this time, with a woman; she seems like a half-done as well. She is very physically fit, and has a symmetrical face. Together, Bill and the woman enter the bed, and get under the covers. I’m not sure what they are doing now, but they are moving around a lot under there…it eventually subsides, and both lie still, asleep. 

The woman is here a lot now, almost every day, and nearly every night. Bill seems to have a strong attachment to her. I can see why, she is quite “hot”—I learned this word from Bill, as he said it to the woman. Her face flushed when he said it.

Weeks go by, now months. One day, Bill shows up to the room, with water in his eyes. Alone. The woman is not with him. His emotions are running amok, it seems. He sits on the bed, turns, and looks at me. He reaches into the drawer below me, and pulls out a fifth of whiskey. Analyzing it, he unscrews the cap, then reaches for me. Popping open my top, he pours from the fifth into my body. The feeling of the warm liquid inside me is quite a new sensation. I am now full; I feel heavier, somehow burdened. My top still open, Bill lifts me to eye level. 

“Just a little something to take the edge off,” he says, and knocks me back. 

\*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

It feels good to finally be drunk from, to finally be used for my purpose. But a week goes by until Bill drinks from me again. After that, another week. Then, a month. He seems a bit happier when he comes into the room. One day, though, he walks in, looking angry. “Fuck this class, fucking bullshit!” he screams. He tears a paper in half and lies down in his bed. He then looks over at me. He grabs me, and drinks from me. After five minutes, I am empty. He refills me, and drains me again. He refills me again, drinks a bit, then falls asleep.

There is another girl in his life now, quite “hot”; a lot like the last girl. Bill seems happy again. He still drinks from me, sometimes. I miss that day where he drank deeply from me; it felt good to be used for my purpose. And, it seems to me that when he is in a bad mood, after drinking from me, he feels better, or is at least able to sleep restfully. I feel I am helping him in those situations, somehow.

A year goes by, and another. During those years, he drinks from me occasionally…but, consistently. He seems to be sad a lot. There doesn’t seem to be a reason, most of the time. The girl still comes to the room most nights. Another year goes by; Bill takes me somewhere new. It looks like a different house, from the outside. He takes me to the bedroom inside, and sets me on the bedside table. His girl is there too.

“This is it babe, what do you think?” Bill says excitedly.

“I love it, Bill. I love you.”

“I love you too, Sarah.”
 
\*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

I can’t see right now, but I can feel a ba-BOOM, ba-BOOM, ba-BOOM. I am inside some kind of cloth pouch. I just woke up from my nap. 

“Do you, Bill Michaels, take Sarah Anderson to be your lawfully wedded wife?” I hear a voice say.

“I do.” Bill says this. Based on where his voice came from, I’m guessing I am in his breast pocket.

More things are said, and then we are moving outside. Bill gets in a car, the car drives for some time, and we arrive at the next destination. Inside, loud music plays. People are talking, and it sounds like a grand time.

“A double shot please,” I hear Bill say. The sound of glass hitting Formica. The sound of liquid going down Bill’s throat. Five minutes pass. He asks for the same thing. Then again, ten minutes later. Then, five minutes later, again. He seems to be stumbling now. I cannot understand his words. People are saying things like “are you OK?” and “are you feeling alright?” Then, he falls, landing facedown, and I am between the floor and his body. 

\*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

“I can’t stand you right now!”

“I can’t stand you either!”

“You are a slob, and you need to clean up your act, or I am leaving you, Bill.”

“You can’t leave me! You’ll never find anyone who loves you like I do, and you know it.”

“Try me.”

Bill and Sarah are standing in the bedroom, screaming at each other about something or another. At this point, Bill seems angry. He rears back his arm, balls up his hand, and lunges forward—right at Sarah’s face. She drops, and begins to cry. In the other room, I hear the sound of a young child crying. It is their child, a boy, named Nathaniel. I feel sorry for them all right now; their life together seems to be falling apart before my eyes.

The next day, I see Sarah leave with Nathaniel. One week goes by. I haven’t seen them again. Another month. It’s just me and Bill, alone in the house, with nothing but the silence to keep us company.

\*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

He fills me again and again. After Sarah left, it started with every few days. But now, it’s daily. Before, when he was sad and would drink from me, he would seem better afterwards. These days, he doesn’t seem any different before versus after taking a drink. I don’t feel like I am helping anymore. I feel like I am hurting. He cries now after drinking from me. He never used to do that.

The bottles pile high, as the months pile up. More months have gone by than I can even count…Bill’s hair is starting to change color a bit. His skin is wrinkling. His body is changing…he seems weaker.

I hate to see this happen to him. I need to figure out a way to stop him drinking from me. I can’t take seeing him this way anymore. I poke a hole in my bottom, so the liquid drains from me. This way, he will not be able to use me to drink, I think.

The next day, he sees that I am empty, and goes to fill me. The liquid drains from the hole in my bottom. “Damnit,” he says. Going to the drawer, he grabs some duct tape, and places a strip on the hole. The liquid stops flowing. He drinks. It’s hopeless.

\*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

One day, he seems to be sadder than ever before. There is just something about his face that is different than any other day I have known him. Hopelessness…that’s the word for it. He grabs me, and goes to the couch in his living room. I am in his left hand; to his right, I see an odd-looking “L” shaped item. It is silver, metallic, and has a hole at the end. Interesting, I think. I’ve never seen that before.

For the next hour, he drinks from me freely. He must have filled me up a half a dozen times; I have since lost count. The hours drag on. It is dark outside; it is raining. Thunder roars; lightning crackles. He picks up the “L” shaped thing. Me in his left hand and the thing in his right, he raises the thing to just below his chin. He points the end with the hole upwards. His finger moves, and pulls a lever. A loud noise. Red everywhere. I fall into a box beside the couch. I have no idea what just happened…but I am saddened, somehow. 

\*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

Some people came to the house to remove the body. I don’t know who they were. Months go by until someone shows up again. I hear several knocks on the door. Someone enters, and they wander throughout the place. Eventually, they make it back around to the living room. They open the box I am in.

The man looks familiar, his eyes resemble Bill’s…his hair, the color Sarah’s was. Nathaniel, I think. He has grown. 

He reaches down, and grabs me. He studies me carefully. He pockets me. 

\*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; \*&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;

I am in a room with Nathaniel. Crying, he reaches for me. His tears subsiding, he pours a drink into me. Then, he speaks, quietly, softly.

“Just a little something to take the edge off.”
